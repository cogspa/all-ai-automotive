Brake Pads & Rotors Quick Guide

Brake Pads & Rotors Installation Overview

Proper brake pad and rotor replacement is essential for vehicle safety and optimal braking performance. This guide provides comprehensive instructions for diagnosing, removing, and installing brake pads and rotors according to industry best practices.

Brake pads and rotors are critical wear items in your vehicle's braking system. The pads provide the friction surface that presses against the rotors (discs) when the brakes are applied, converting kinetic energy into heat energy to slow and stop the vehicle.

Following these best practices during installation will help ensure proper brake system function, maximize component life, and prevent issues such as noise, vibration, premature wear, or reduced braking performance.

Important Safety Note:

The brake system is a critical safety component. Always follow manufacturer procedures and take extra precautions during installation. If you're unsure about any aspect of brake service, consult a professional technician.

Vehicle Preparation

  1. 1
    Refer to vehicle service information for any special testing, removal, or installation procedures
    • Check for any related technical service bulletins
  2. 2
    Obtain all vehicle information to ensure correct part application
    • Make, year, model, engine, drivetrain configuration (FWD, RWD, 4WD, AWD), brake system details, etc.
  3. 3
    Test drive vehicle to verify customer brake system complaint (if applicable)
    • Note any noise, vibration, pulling, or pedal feedback
    • Test braking performance under various conditions (light/heavy braking, low/high speeds)
  4. 4
    Raise and safely support vehicle. Refer to vehicle service info for lifting/jacking instructions
    • Ensure vehicle is on level ground before lifting
    • Use proper jack points and jack stands rated for the vehicle weight
  5. 5
    Remove all 4 wheels and perform a complete brake system inspection
    • Inspect underhood brake system components – master cylinder, brake fluid condition
    • Complete inspection of the tires, steering, suspension, and driveline components for wear or damage should be performed as problems in these areas can sometimes affect brake performance
    • Check for uneven pad wear, rotor scoring, discoloration, or warping
    • Inspect brake fluid level and condition

Brake Disassembly

  1. 1
    Remove brake calipers (and caliper brackets if applicable)
    • Note: If servicing rear brakes and the vehicle is equipped with electric parking brakes, the system will need to be placed into the service mode before disassembly (refer to vehicle service info)
    • Properly support brake calipers. Do not allow brake calipers to hang from brake hose
    • Using a brake hose clamp tool, clamp off brake hose, open bleeder screw on brake caliper
    • Using the correct tool, depress and seat caliper piston(s) into caliper housing bore
    • Close bleeder screw and remove brake hose clamp tool
  2. 2
    Remove brake pads
    • Note the orientation and position of any anti-rattle clips or hardware
    • Inspect removed pads for unusual wear patterns that may indicate other issues
  3. 3
    Remove brake rotors
    • Some rotors may require removal of a retaining screw
    • Rotors may require light tapping with a mallet to break them loose from the hub
  4. 4
    Thoroughly clean hub to rotor mounting surface to remove rust, corrosion, oxidation
    • Use a wire brush or suitable cleaning tool
    • Ensure mounting surface is completely clean for proper rotor seating
  5. 5
    Thoroughly clean and service brake caliper brackets (if applicable) to remove rust, dirt, corrosion
    • Pay special attention to caliper slider pins and bushings
    • Ensure smooth movement of all sliding components

Brake Reassembly

  1. 1
    Wash brake rotors with soap and water to remove any contaminants
    • Ensure rotors are completely dry before installation
    • Do not use petroleum-based cleaners on brake components
  2. 2
    Install new brake rotors
    • Ensure proper seating on hub mounting surface
    • Install any retaining screws if applicable
  3. 3
    Apply high-temperature brake grease to caliper bracket contact points
    • Apply grease only to manufacturer-specified contact points
    • Do not over-apply grease as excess can attract dirt and debris
  4. 4
    Install new brake pads
    • Ensure proper orientation and positioning
    • Install all anti-rattle clips and hardware in correct positions
    • Verify pads move freely in caliper bracket
  5. 5
    Install brake calipers and caliper brackets
    • Torque all mounting hardware to manufacturer specifications
    • Verify caliper moves freely on slide pins
    • Check that brake hoses are not twisted or kinked

Completion & Testing

  1. 1
    Check brake fluid level and top off as needed
    • Use only the correct type of brake fluid specified for the vehicle
    • Check for any signs of fluid leaks
  2. 2
    Install wheels and torque lug nuts to specification
    • Use a star pattern when tightening lug nuts
    • Verify wheels rotate freely without interference
  3. 3
    Lower vehicle and perform brake system checks
    • Pump brake pedal several times to seat pads against rotors
    • Check for firm pedal feel before moving vehicle
    • Verify parking brake operation (if rear brakes were serviced)
  4. 4
    Take electric parking brake system out of service mode (if applicable)
    • Use scan tool to deactivate service mode
    • Test parking brake function
  5. 5
    Perform initial brake test and road test
    • Start with low-speed stops in a safe area
    • Gradually increase speed and braking force
    • Listen for any unusual noises
    • Check for proper brake operation and pedal feel
    • Verify no warning lights are illuminated
  6. 6
    Final inspection and documentation
    • Recheck all fasteners for proper torque
    • Verify no fluid leaks at any connection points
    • Document work performed and any recommendations for customer
    • Advise customer of break-in procedure requirements
